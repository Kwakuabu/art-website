Our Work

A sample of what we have delivered.

Organisational Research 5 weeks · Quantitative

Mobile Money Adoption and SME Growth in Greater Accra

A quantitative study examining how mobile payment platforms changed operations across 220 small enterprises in Accra, La Dade Kotopon, and Tema. Findings were shared with fintech providers and used in district-level policy discussions.

Academic Research 8 weeks · Mixed Methods

Digital Financial Inclusion in Rural Volta Region

A mixed-methods undergraduate dissertation exploring barriers to digital banking adoption among rural communities. The study combined household surveys with in-depth interviews across four districts.

Development Research 6 weeks · Pre-Post Design

Nutrition Intervention Impact Assessment, Northern Region

An impact evaluation conducted for an NGO running a community nutrition programme. Measured outcomes across 12 communities using pre and post intervention data collection.

Business Research 3 weeks · Survey & Desk

Agri-Tech Market Sizing for Smallholder Farmer Platform

A feasibility and market sizing study for a proposed digital platform targeting smallholder farmers in three regions. Included competitor mapping, willingness-to-pay analysis, and demand projections.

Academic Research MSc Thesis · Machine Learning

A Machine Learning Framework for Threat Detection in Electronic Medical Records Systems

A design science study validating four ML algorithms — Random Forest, XGBoost, SVM, and MLP — against both 1,500 synthetic and 476 real EMR access logs from 37 Military Hospital. XGBoost led on synthetic data; Random Forest and XGBoost exceeded 99% F1-scores on real-world validation, with findings informing deployment guidance for healthcare EMR security.

Academic Research BRSET · Differential Privacy

Privacy-Utility-Fairness Trilemma in Ophthalmological Machine Learning

A differential privacy evaluation on 14,280 retinal records from the BRSET dataset, testing four classifiers under Laplace and Gaussian mechanisms across 15 epsilon levels. ε=2.0 with Laplace achieved clinically viable utility (MLP macro F1=0.6957, AUC=0.9320), while a structural fairness gap of 0.317 F1 points was found to be prevalence-driven rather than privacy-induced.
